Frequently asked

Why does Alloy live in the menu bar?

Alloy is a background utility. It keeps a small icon in the menu bar for an at-a-glance battery read-out and opens its full dashboard when you click it.

Does Alloy send my data anywhere?

No. Everything stays on your Mac. See the Privacy Policy.

How do I start Alloy automatically at login?

Open Alloy's Settings and turn on “Launch at login”.

The energy numbers look approximate — why?

On the Mac App Store, macOS sandboxing prevents apps from reading the exact per-app CPU time of other processes, so Alloy estimates each app's energy impact from system activity and which app is active. It is a useful relative ranking, not an exact per-app measurement.

How do I remove my data?

Delete history from Alloy's settings, or uninstall the app to remove everything it stored on your Mac.
