Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2 July 2026

Alloy collects no data and makes no network connections. Everything it does happens entirely on your Mac.

What Alloy accesses

Alloy reads your Mac's battery information (charge, health, cycle count, temperature) and the CPU/energy usage of running apps in order to show you battery status, energy insights, and weekly reports.

Where your data goes

Nowhere. All readings, history, and settings are stored locally on your Mac (in the app's on-device storage). Alloy has no server, no account, and no analytics. It does not include the network entitlement, so it is technically unable to transmit any information off your device.

Data collection

  • No personal data is collected.
  • No usage analytics or telemetry.
  • No advertising or tracking.
  • No third-party SDKs.
  • No in-app purchases.

Notifications

If you enable notifications, Alloy schedules local alerts (e.g. low battery, high charge, overheating) through macOS. These are generated and delivered entirely on your device.

Data deletion

Because all data stays on your Mac, removing it is entirely in your control: delete history from within Alloy's settings, or uninstall the app to remove everything it stored.

Children's privacy

Alloy is a utility that collects no data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of Alloy changes what it accesses or transmits, this policy will be updated before that version is released.
